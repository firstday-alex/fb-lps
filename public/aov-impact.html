AOV Impact Analysis

Loads Shopify orders for a date range and the previous period, attributes by customerJourney first-visit UTM, and decomposes Δ Revenue + Δ Blended AOV across channels.

Channel Revenue Decomposition

Each channel's contribution to Δ Revenue, split into: Orders Effect = ΔOrders × prevAOV (more/fewer orders at the old basket), AOV Effect = prevOrders × ΔAOV (same orders earning a higher/lower basket), and Interaction = ΔOrders × ΔAOV (the cross-term). The three sum to Δ Revenue for that channel; rows sum to the overall Δ Revenue.

Why is AOV moving?  (units/order × price/unit)

AOV is identical to UPT × ASP: units per transaction (basket size) times average selling price per unit. UPT Effect = ΔUPT × prevASP (basket-size move at the old price). ASP Effect = prevUPT × ΔASP (per-unit price move at the old basket). Interaction = ΔUPT × ΔASP. The three sum to Δ AOV for that channel. If UPT Effect dominates → people are buying fewer items per order. If ASP Effect dominates → average unit price fell (discounts or mix-shift to cheaper SKUs).

Blended AOV Decomposition

Decomposes the change in blended AOV (overall revenue ÷ overall orders) into per-channel contributions. Own-AOV Effect = prevOrderShare × ΔAOV (this channel's own AOV move, weighted by its share of last period's orders). Mix-Shift Effect = ΔOrderShare × (currAOV − blended avg) (gaining/losing orders at a basket above or below the blended average). The two sum to this channel's contribution; rows sum to total Δ Blended AOV.
